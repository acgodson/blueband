The **2023 ICC Men's Cricket World Cup** (also referred to as simply the **2023 Cricket World Cup**) was the 13th edition of the [Cricket World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cricket_World_Cup "Cricket World Cup"), a quadrennial [One Day International](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_Day_International "One Day International") (ODI) [cricket](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cricket "Cricket") tournament organized by the [International Cricket Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Cricket_Council "International Cricket Council") (ICC). It was hosted from 5 October to 19 November 2023 across ten venues in [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India").

The tournament was contested by ten national teams, maintaining the same format used in [2019](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019_Cricket_World_Cup "2019 Cricket World Cup"). In the knockout stage, India and Australia beat New Zealand and South Africa respectively to advance to the final, played on 19 November at [Narendra Modi Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Narendra_Modi_Stadium "Narendra Modi Stadium"). Australia won by 6 wickets, winning their sixth Cricket World Cup title.

[Virat Kohli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virat_Kohli "Virat Kohli") was the player of the tournament and also scored the most runs; [Mohammed Shami](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohammed_Shami "Mohammed Shami") was the leading wicket-taker. A total of 1,250,307 spectators attended matches, the highest number in any Cricket World Cup to-date.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-1) The tournament final set viewership records in India, with 518 million viewers, and a peak of 57 million streaming viewers.

Background
----------

On 11 December 2017, India was announced by the ICC as hosts of the 2023 Cricket World Cup; while India had served as a co-host during three previous tournaments (most recently in [2011](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011_Cricket_World_Cup "2011 Cricket World Cup"), which it co-hosted with Sri Lanka and Bangladesh), it would mark the first Cricket World Cup to be hosted solely by India.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-2)

Originally, the competition was to be played from 9 February to 26 March 2023.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-3)[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-Dates-4) In July 2020 it was announced that due to the disruption of the qualification schedule by the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic_on_cricket "Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on cricket"), the start of the tournament would be delayed to October.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-icc_postpone-5)[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-6) The ICC released the tournament schedule on 27 June 2023.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-Fixtures-7)[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-8)

The [Pakistan Cricket Board](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan_Cricket_Board "Pakistan Cricket Board") (PCB) had threatened to boycott the tournament after the [Board of Control for Cricket in India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Board_of_Control_for_Cricket_in_India "Board of Control for Cricket in India") (BCCI) refused to send a team to the [2023 Asia Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Asia_Cup "2023 Asia Cup") scheduled in Pakistan.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-9)[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-10) This issue was resolved in June 2023 after the [Asian Cricket Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_Cricket_Council "Asian Cricket Council") announced that the tournament would be hosted using a hybrid model proposed by the PCB, with nine of the 13 matches in the competition played in Sri Lanka.[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-11)[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-12)

This was the first ICC World Cup in which penalties for slow over-rates were given to bowling sides if they did not complete their 50 overs in the stipulated time. On-field umpires could penalise the bowling team by not allowing more than four fielders outside the 30-yard circle.[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-13)

Main article: [2023 Cricket World Cup qualification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup_qualification "2023 Cricket World Cup qualification")

Other than India, who qualified as hosts, all teams had to qualify for the tournament through the [2023 Cricket World Cup qualification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup_qualification "2023 Cricket World Cup qualification") process. Afghanistan, Australia, Bangladesh, England, New Zealand, Pakistan and South Africa qualified via the [ICC Cricket World Cup Super League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020%E2%80%932023_ICC_Cricket_World_Cup_Super_League "2020–2023 ICC Cricket World Cup Super League"), with the Netherlands and Sri Lanka securing the final two places via the [2023 Cricket World Cup Qualifier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup_Qualifier "2023 Cricket World Cup Qualifier") in Zimbabwe during June and July 2023.

As a result of the qualifying process, the competition was the first not to include former winners [West Indies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Indies_cricket_team "West Indies cricket team"), who failed to progress for the first time after their defeat to [Scotland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scotland_national_cricket_team "Scotland national cricket team").[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-Witney-6Jul2023-14) Full members [Ireland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ireland_cricket_team "Ireland cricket team") and [Zimbabwe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe_national_cricket_team "Zimbabwe national cricket team") also missed out on qualification, meaning three of the four [full members](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_International_Cricket_Council_members#Full_Members "List of International Cricket Council members") who took part in the knock-out qualification stage did not qualify, with only [Sri Lanka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lanka_national_cricket_team "Sri Lanka national cricket team") progressing.[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-AP-6Jul23-15) The final qualification spot was decided by an eliminator match between associate members Scotland and [the Netherlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netherlands_national_cricket_team "Netherlands national cricket team"),[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-16) with the Dutch side taking the final place.[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-Witney-6Jul2023-14)

2023 Cricket World Cup venues

The tournament took place in ten different stadiums, situated in ten different cities across India. The first and second semi-finals were held at [Wankhede Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wankhede_Stadium "Wankhede Stadium") in [Mumbai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mumbai "Mumbai") and [Eden Gardens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eden_Gardens "Eden Gardens") in [Kolkata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kolkata "Kolkata") respectively, while the final took place at [Narendra Modi Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Narendra_Modi_Stadium "Narendra Modi Stadium") in [Ahmedabad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmedabad "Ahmedabad").[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-Fixtures-7)

The BCCI provided funding for renovations and refurbishments at stadiums. [Himachal Pradesh Cricket Association Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Himachal_Pradesh_Cricket_Association_Stadium "Himachal Pradesh Cricket Association Stadium") received a new grass surface, drainage system, seating, and hospitality boxes. Wankhede Stadium had upgrades to the outfield, floodlights, corporate boxes, and toilets. [M. A. Chidambaram Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M._A._Chidambaram_Stadium "M. A. Chidambaram Stadium") installed new floodlights and relaid two wickets.[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-17)

With the autumn scheduling of this World Cup, the ICC instituted protocols for reducing the impact of moisture—including [dew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dew "Dew") and rain—on pitch conditions, so that they did not give the team batting second an advantage (as had frequently occurred in the [2021 Men's T20 World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2021_ICC_Men%27s_T20_World_Cup "2021 ICC Men's T20 World Cup")). These included using a specific wetting agent, and the boundary set at around 70 m (77 yards) at each stadium, with more grass on the pitch to encourage [seam bowling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seam_bowling "Seam bowling") over [spin bowling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin_bowling "Spin bowling").[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-18)

Squads
------

Main article: [2023 Cricket World Cup squads](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup_squads "2023 Cricket World Cup squads")

All teams were asked to finalise their 15-player squads prior to 28 September, with any replacements after this date requiring approval from the ICC.[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-20) All squads were announced by 26 September 2023.[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-21) The oldest player of the tournament was Dutch player [Wesley Barresi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wesley_Barresi "Wesley Barresi"), who was 39 years old, while the youngest was Afghan spinner [Noor Ahmad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noor_Ahmad "Noor Ahmad"), who was 18.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-22)

Match officials
---------------

Main article: [2023 Cricket World Cup officials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup_officials "2023 Cricket World Cup officials")

.mw-parser-output .excerpt-hat .mw-editsection-like{font-style:normal}

Match officials for the 2023 Cricket World Cup were selected by the ICC's umpire selection panel, headed by Sean Easey, the ICC's senior manager for umpires and referees. The panel selected 12 umpires to officiate at the tournament: three from Australia, four from England, four from Asia (one each from Bangladesh, India, Pakistan and Sri Lanka), two each from New Zealand and South Africa and one from the West Indies. It also selected four [match referees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Match_referee "Match referee") for the event.[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-2023_Cricket_World_Cup_officials_2023_Officials-23)[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-24)

Warm-up matches
---------------

Warm-up matches were held from 29 September to 3 October 2023 at [Rajiv Gandhi International Cricket Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rajiv_Gandhi_International_Cricket_Stadium "Rajiv Gandhi International Cricket Stadium") in [Hyderabad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyderabad "Hyderabad"), [Assam Cricket Association Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assam_Cricket_Association_Stadium,_Guwahati "Assam Cricket Association Stadium, Guwahati") in [Guwahati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guwahati "Guwahati"), and [Greenfield International Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenfield_International_Stadium "Greenfield International Stadium") in [Thiruvananthapuram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiruvananthapuram "Thiruvananthapuram").[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-Fixtures-7)

India's warm-up fixtures were announced on 27 June. The complete warm-up fixtures were announced on 23 August.[\[25\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-25) The matches were broadcast live on television.[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-:0-26)[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-27)

Group stage
-----------

Main article: [2023 Cricket World Cup group stage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup_group_stage "2023 Cricket World Cup group stage")

The ICC announced the World Cup schedule on 27 June 2023 at an event in Mumbai with a countdown of 100 days to the opening match of the World Cup on 5 October. The group stage started with the match between the [finalists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019_Cricket_World_Cup_final "2019 Cricket World Cup final") of the [2019 Cricket World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019_Cricket_World_Cup "2019 Cricket World Cup"), [New Zealand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Zealand_national_cricket_team "New Zealand national cricket team") and [England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England_cricket_team "England cricket team"), at [Narendra Modi Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Narendra_Modi_Stadium "Narendra Modi Stadium").[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-Fixtures-7) On 9 August 2023, nine fixtures, including the match between India and Pakistan, were rescheduled by the ICC.[\[28\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-28) The top seven teams in the tournament, excluding Pakistan who qualify automatically as host, qualified for the [2025 ICC Champions Trophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_ICC_Champions_Trophy "2025 ICC Champions Trophy").[\[29\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-29)

Knockout stage
--------------

Main article: [2023 Cricket World Cup knockout stage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup_knockout_stage "2023 Cricket World Cup knockout stage")

The host [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India_national_cricket_team "India national cricket team") was the first team to qualify for the semi-finals after their 302-run win against [Sri Lanka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lanka_national_cricket_team "Sri Lanka national cricket team"), their seventh successive win in the World Cup.[\[31\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-31) India secured the top place amongst the semi-finalists after they beat [South Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa_national_cricket_team "South Africa national cricket team") by 243 runs on 5 November at [Eden Gardens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eden_Gardens "Eden Gardens") in [Kolkata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kolkata "Kolkata").[\[32\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-32)

South Africa became the second team to qualify for the semi-finals after [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan_national_cricket_team "Pakistan national cricket team") defeated [New Zealand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Zealand_national_cricket_team "New Zealand national cricket team") on 4 November,[\[33\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-33) with [Australia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australia_national_cricket_team "Australia national cricket team") becoming the third team to qualify after defeating [Afghanistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afghanistan_national_cricket_team "Afghanistan national cricket team") on 7 November.[\[34\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-34) New Zealand confirmed their berth as the fourth team after Pakistan lost their final match against [England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England_national_cricket_team "England national cricket team").[\[35\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-35)

Marketing
---------

The ICC hosted a "trophy tour" for 100 days prior to the tournament beginning 27 June, with the [Cricket World Cup Trophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cricket_World_Cup_Trophy "Cricket World Cup Trophy") being taken to various locations around the world. The event began with the launching of the trophy into the [stratosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stratosphere "Stratosphere") and landing at Modi Stadium—becoming the first sports trophy to have ever been sent into space.[\[40\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-40)

The ICC officially announced the mascots for the World Cup in August. The mascots were a male and female duo named "Tonk" and "Blaze" from the fictional cricketing utopia "Crictoverse".[\[41\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-41)[\[42\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-42)

The official theme song of the 2023 Cricket World Cup titled "Dil Jashn Bole" (transl. Heart say celebrate) was released on 20 September. The song was composed by [Pritam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pritam "Pritam"), with lyrics by Shloke Lal and Saaveri Verma. The song was sung by Pritam, [Nakash Aziz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nakash_Aziz "Nakash Aziz"), [Sreerama Chandra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sreerama_Chandra "Sreerama Chandra"), [Amit Mishra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amit_Mishra_(singer) "Amit Mishra (singer)"), [Jonita Gandhi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonita_Gandhi "Jonita Gandhi"), [Akasa Singh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akasa_Singh "Akasa Singh") and [S. P. Charan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S._P._Charan "S. P. Charan").[\[43\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-43) However, the song was subject to backlash and bad reviews.[\[44\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-44)

Ahead of the tournament, it was reported that an opening ceremony would take place on 4 October 2023 at the [Narendra Modi Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Narendra_Modi_Stadium "Narendra Modi Stadium") in [Ahmedabad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmedabad "Ahmedabad"), a day before the opening match at the same venue.[\[45\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-45) The ceremony was cancelled and replaced by a closing ceremony ahead of the final.[\[46\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-46) During this a [drone show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drone_art "Drone art") was held.[\[47\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-47)[\[48\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-48)

Broadcasting
------------

[Disney Star](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disney_Star "Disney Star") served as host broadcaster of the tournament in association with ICC TV;[\[49\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-svge-vertical-49) in India, all matches were televised by [Star Sports](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Sports_(Indian_TV_network) "Star Sports (Indian TV network)") and streamed by [Disney+ Hotstar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disney%2B_Hotstar "Disney+ Hotstar"), with coverage available in English and eight [regional languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Languages_of_India "Languages of India").[\[50\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-50) Amid competition with [JioCinema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JioCinema "JioCinema") for domestic cricket rights,[\[51\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-:1-51)[\[52\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-:2-52) Disney announced that all matches would be available on Disney+ Hotstar for free on mobile devices. The broadcasts featured expanded player and [Hawk-Eye](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawk-Eye "Hawk-Eye") ball tracking features (building upon those introduced in the [2022 men's T20 World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_ICC_Men%27s_T20_World_Cup "2022 ICC Men's T20 World Cup")) for visualizing shots and fielding, and dedicated [vertical video](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_video "Vertical video") feeds of each match designed for streaming on [smartphones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smartphone "Smartphone"). Commentary on the ICC's English-language world feed was led by [Ricky Ponting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ricky_Ponting "Ricky Ponting") and [Eoin Morgan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eoin_Morgan "Eoin Morgan") among others.[\[51\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-:1-51)[\[49\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-svge-vertical-49)

The ICC projected that global live viewing minutes of the tournament had increased by 17% over 2019.[\[53\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-53) In India, [Broadcast Audience Research Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadcast_Audience_Research_Council "Broadcast Audience Research Council") (BARC) ratings reported that the final was seen on [linear television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_television "Linear television") by 518 million people in India, and Disney reported that streams peaked at 57 million concurrent viewers on Hotstar—both setting new records.[\[52\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-:2-52)

Prize money
-----------

The ICC allocated a pool of US$10 million in prize money for the tournament, with payouts remaining the same as the 2019 and 2015 tournaments. Australia, the winning team, received US$4,000,000, the runner-up $2,000,000 and the losing semi-finalists $1,600,000. Teams that did not progress past the league stage received $100,000 and the winner of each league stage match received $40,000.[\[54\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-54)[\[55\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Cricket_World_Cup#cite_note-55)
